1. Introduction

This portfolio explores the virtual and physical development of a motorised lion-themed exoskeletal helmet. Tools like Fusion360, Blender, and 3D scanning were integral, alongside workshops focused on composites and motion capture. This documentation reflects on iterative workflows and practical insight into technical implementations.

2. Module Overview & Objectives

The module introduced workflows transforming digital concepts into functional artefacts, emphasising digital-to-physical design. It covered CAD software, motion capture, 3D scanning, and fabrication techniques. Students critically assessed each tool for industrial use and product relevance.

3. Technologies Explored

3D Scanning

Used to replicate organic shapes, enabling accurate helmet fitting and reference geometry for further modelling.

Motion Capture

Enabled analysis of natural human motion, guiding design decisions for motorised components such as eyelid actuation.

Digital Fabrication

Combining 3D printing, laser cutting, and resin moulding, the helmet was brought from digital prototype to tangible form.
